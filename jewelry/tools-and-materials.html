Tools

A guide to common jewelry-making tools

Round Nose Pliers

These are good for shaping wire,
but not good for gripping your project

Where to buy

Needle Nose Pliers

These pliers are usually used
to grip your project while
working and don't do so well
with shaping wire.

Where to buy

3-in-1 Pliers

Some stores will also offer a 3-
in-1 tool. while they are a
little cheaper than buying the
set of 3, I don't recommend
them. Although, if you're in a
pinch, they'll do.

Where to buy

Wire Cutters

Just as the name suggests- they
are for cutting wire. While there
are many places that sell wire
cutters for jewelry-making, what I
really recommend are wire
cutters meant for floral crafts.
They usually take less effort than
the ones for jewelry-making and
give cleaner cuts.

Wire

Also sold in spools at your local craft store. For jewelry-making, a gauge around 18–24 is recommended. When choosing your wire, keep in mind that the lower the gauge, the harder it will be to shape — but it will last longer and be more durable.

Where to Buy

Jump Rings

Small rings made from wire. They are used for
many different purposes like connecting elements
and holding charms.

Where to buy

Lobster Clasps

Meant for bracelets and necklaces. If you've
worn necklaces before they will look very
familiar to you.

Where to buy

Head Pin and Eye Pins

Both are used with beads. Head pins have a
stopper on one side meant to stop beads from
falling off. Eye pins are similar, but have a loop
instead of a stopper so that the segment can
be attached on both sides. I do not recommend
buying these online as it is hard to determine
the quality you will be receiving.

Eye Pins Head Pins

Fishhooks

Earring hooks meant for dangle-earrings.
The loop on the end is good for attaching
jump rings, chain, eye-pins, and more.
Depending on the brand and type you
get, the loop may or may not be able to
open.

Where to buy

Tip: When choosing beads and other materials, make sure they are compatible.
All too many times I have chosen wire too thick for the beads I got or the hole in
my bead was too large for my head-pin and falls off.
